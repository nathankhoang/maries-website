Vietnamese Pronouns

In Vietnamese there are no pronouns equivalent to "I" and "You" as used in English. The pronouns you use will depend on the patient's age and your relationship with them. We also typically place the pronoun in front of someone's name when addressing them, especially when they are older, as a sign of respect.

In the clinical translations, "bạn" (friend) will be a placeholder for the appropriate pronoun.
